## ðŸ“Š Interview Process

### Round 1: Phone Screen (30-45 min)

**What to Expect:**
- Coding problem (LeetCode Easy-Medium)
- Discussion of past projects
- Cultural fit questions

**Example Questions:**
- Build a simple REST API
- Implement a feature from scratch
- Debug a code snippet
- Explain a technical decision

**Tips:**
- Show enthusiasm for the product
- Ask about tech stack
- Demonstrate learning ability
- Be conversational

---

### Round 2: Technical Interview (60-90 min)

**Focus Areas:**
- **Practical Coding** (50%) - Build real features
- **System Design** (30%) - Scale their product
- **Debugging** (20%) - Fix production issues

**Example Tasks:**
1. **Build a Feature:**
   - "Implement user authentication"
   - "Add real-time notifications"
   - "Create a payment flow"

2. **System Design:**
   - "Scale our API to 1M users"
   - "Design a webhook system"
   - "Implement rate limiting"

3. **Debugging:**
   - "This endpoint is slow, why?"
   - "Users report data inconsistency"
   - "Memory leak in production"

---

### Round 3: Founder/CTO Round (45-60 min)

**What They Look For:**
- **Ownership** - Take initiative
- **Scrappiness** - Do more with less
- **Learning** - Adapt quickly
- **Impact** - Ship fast
- **Culture** - Team fit

**Common Questions:**
- Why this startup?
- What excites you about our product?
- Tell me about a time you shipped fast
- How do you handle ambiguity?
- What's your biggest technical achievement?

**Tips:**
- Research the company deeply
- Use their product extensively
- Prepare thoughtful questions
- Show genuine excitement
- Discuss their challenges

## ðŸ’¡ Startup-Specific Questions

### Product Thinking

**Q:** "How would you improve our product?"

**Good Answer:**
- Used the product extensively
- Identified specific pain points
- Proposed data-driven solutions
- Considered trade-offs
- Aligned with company goals

**Example:**
"I noticed the onboarding flow has 5 steps. Based on my experience, users drop off at step 3 (payment). I'd suggest:
1. Move payment to the end
2. Add social proof at step 3
3. A/B test the new flow
4. Measure conversion improvement"

---

### Technical Decisions

**Q:** "Should we use microservices or monolith?"

**Good Answer:**
- Consider team size
- Evaluate complexity
- Discuss trade-offs
- Recommend based on stage

**Example:**
"For a 10-person team, I'd start with a well-structured monolith:
- Faster development
- Easier debugging
- Lower operational overhead
- Can extract services later when needed

Microservices make sense when:
- Team > 50 people
- Clear service boundaries
- Independent scaling needs"

## ðŸ’° Compensation

### Equity Breakdown

**Early Stage (Seed/Series A):**
- **Equity:** 0.5% - 2%
- **Base:** $100K - $150K
- **Risk:** High
- **Upside:** 10-100x potential

**Growth Stage (Series B/C):**
- **Equity:** 0.1% - 0.5%
- **Base:** $130K - $180K
- **Risk:** Medium
- **Upside:** 3-10x potential

**Late Stage (Series D+):**
- **Equity:** 0.05% - 0.2%
- **Base:** $150K - $200K
- **Risk:** Low
- **Upside:** 2-5x potential

---

### Equity Considerations

**Questions to Ask:**
1. What's the current valuation?
2. How many shares outstanding?
3. What's my % ownership?
4. What's the vesting schedule? (typically 4 years, 1-year cliff)
5. What's the strike price?
6. 409A valuation?
7. Liquidation preferences?

**Red Flags:**
- Won't disclose valuation
- Unusual vesting (>4 years)
- High strike price
- Multiple liquidation preferences

## üíª Technical Phone Screen

### Format (45-60 min)
- 5 min: Introductions
- 35-45 min: Coding problem
- 5-10 min: Your questions

### Common Problems
- Array/String manipulation
- Hash tables
- Two pointers
- Binary search
- Tree traversal
- BFS/DFS

### Tips
1. **Clarify Requirements**
   - Ask about input size
   - Edge cases
   - Expected output format

2. **Think Out Loud**
   - Explain your approach
   - Discuss trade-offs
   - Mention alternatives

3. **Start Simple**
   - Brute force first
   - Then optimize
   - Explain complexity

4. **Test Your Code**
   - Walk through examples
   - Check edge cases
   - Fix bugs calmly

5. **Optimize**
   - Discuss time/space complexity
   - Suggest improvements
   - Implement if time allows

## üí∞ Salary Negotiation

### Research

**Use:**
- [levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/)
- [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/)
- [Blind](https://www.teamblind.com/)
- [Payscale](https://www.payscale.com/)

**Consider:**
- Location (SF/NYC vs remote)
- Company size
- Your experience level
- Market demand

### Strategy

1. **Never Give First Number**
   - "What's your budget for this role?"
   - "I'm flexible based on total comp"

2. **Get Multiple Offers**
   - Leverage competition
   - "I have another offer"

3. **Negotiate All Components**
   - Base salary
   - Bonus
   - Stock/equity
   - Sign-on bonus
   - Relocation
   - Remote flexibility

4. **Be Professional**
   - Express enthusiasm
   - Be grateful
   - Stay firm but polite
   - Get everything in writing

### Email Template

```
Hi [Recruiter],

Thank you for the offer! I'm excited about the opportunity to join [Company].

After reviewing the offer and considering my experience and the market, I was hoping we could discuss the compensation package.

Based on my research and conversations with peers, I was expecting:
- Base: $X
- Equity: Y shares
- Sign-on: $Z

Is there flexibility in these areas?

I'm very interested in joining the team and hope we can find a package that works for both of us.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## üö´ Common Mistakes

### Technical Interviews

1. **Jumping to Code** - Think first
2. **Not Asking Questions** - Clarify requirements
3. **Silent Coding** - Think out loud
4. **Giving Up** - Ask for hints
5. **Not Testing** - Always test
6. **Ignoring Complexity** - Discuss Big O

### Behavioral Interviews

1. **Vague Answers** - Be specific
2. **No Metrics** - Quantify results
3. **Blaming Others** - Take ownership
4. **Rambling** - Keep it concise (2-3 min)
5. **No Questions** - Always ask questions
